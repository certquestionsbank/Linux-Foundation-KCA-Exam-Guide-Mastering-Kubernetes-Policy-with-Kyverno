The KCA exam focuses on practical and conceptual knowledge of Kyverno and its role in Kubernetes clusters.
As Kubernetes adoption accelerates across enterprises, enforcing consistent policies at scale has become a critical challenge. The Linux Foundation Kyverno Certified Associate (KCA) exam addresses this need by validating foundational knowledge of Kyverno, a Kubernetes-native policy engine designed for security, compliance, and governance. Backed by The Linux Foundation, the KCA certification targets professionals who want to demonstrate their understanding of policy-as-code concepts within cloud-native environments.

## Exam Overview

The KCA exam focuses on practical and conceptual knowledge of Kyverno and its role in Kubernetes clusters. Kyverno operates using Kubernetes-native constructs, allowing teams to define policies without learning complex domain-specific languages.

## Key Objective Analysis

Although official domains are not specified in source, the KCA exam typically centers on core Kyverno and Kubernetes policy concepts, including:

- Understanding Kubernetes resource validation and mutation
- Applying policy-as-code principles
- Using Kyverno for security and compliance enforcement
- Managing policies across namespaces and clusters
- Observing how policies integrate into CI/CD workflows

Candidates are expected to grasp how Kyverno helps automate governance while remaining fully aligned with Kubernetes APIs.

## Reasons to Choose This Exam

The KCA certification is particularly attractive for professionals working in cloud-native environments who want a vendor-neutral credential. Reasons to choose this exam include:

- Relevance to DevOps, DevSecOps, and platform engineering roles

By choosing KCA, candidates show that they understand how to manage policy at scale without introducing unnecessary complexity.
